Probability Theory

Conditional Probability

P(A|B) = P(A∩B) / P(B) - the probability of A given that B has occurred.

Bayes' Theorem

Update beliefs based on new evidence: P(H|E) = P(E|H) × P(H) / P(E)

Hypothesis Testing

Null vs Alternative Hypothesis

H₀ assumes no effect, H₁ assumes there is an effect. We collect evidence to reject or fail to reject H₀.

Type I & Type II Errors

Type I (false positive): Rejecting H₀ when it's true. Type II (false negative): Failing to reject H₀ when it's false.

p-values & Significance

p-value measures evidence against H₀. Conventionally, p < 0.05 suggests statistical significance.

Statistical Distributions

Continuous vs Discrete

Discrete distributions have countable outcomes (binomial, Poisson). Continuous have infinite possible values (normal, exponential).
