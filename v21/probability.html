Probability — Bayes, Puzzles & Distributions

Bayes' theorem, expected value, classic puzzles (Monty Hall), and probability distributions.

1 Bayes' Theorem & Base Rate Fallacy

▾

Bayes' Theorem: P(A|B) = P(B|A) × P(A) / P(B). The key insight is that even a very accurate test can yield mostly false positives when the base rate (prevalence) is low.

Classic Problem

A disease test is 99% accurate (sensitivity and specificity). Disease prevalence is 1%. You test positive. What's P(disease | positive)?

Solution: P(+|D) = 0.99, P(D) = 0.01, P(+|no D) = 0.01, P(no D) = 0.99.

P(D|+) = (0.99 × 0.01) / (0.99 × 0.01 + 0.01 × 0.99) = 0.0099 / 0.0198 = 50%

Despite the test being "99% accurate," a positive result only gives a 50% chance of actually having the disease. The low base rate is the critical factor.

2 Monty Hall & Classic Puzzles

▾

In the Monty Hall problem, you pick a door (1 of 3). The host opens another door revealing a goat. Should you switch?

StrategyWin ProbabilityWhy
Stay1/3Your initial pick had 1/3 chance
Switch2/3The 2/3 probability collapses to the remaining door
Intuition

Always switch. Your initial choice was 1/3 likely correct. The host's reveal doesn't change that; it just concentrates the remaining 2/3 onto one door.

3 Expected Value

▾

Expected value is the long-run average outcome: E[X] = Σ x⋅P(x).

Fair die: E[X] = (1+2+3+4+5+6)/6 = 21/6 = 3.5

DistributionE[X]Var[X]

Interview Tip

Expected value questions are extremely common. Practice computing E[X] for games, bets, and business scenarios.

4 Conditional Probability & Independence

▾

P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B). Two events are independent if P(A|B) = P(A), meaning knowing B tells you nothing about A.

ConceptFormulaMeaning
ConditionalP(A|B) = P(A∩B)/P(B)Probability of A given B occurred
