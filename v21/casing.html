AARRR framework, metric diagnosis, North Star metrics, market sizing, and experiment design.

1 AARRR Pirate Metrics Framework

▾

The AARRR (Pirate Metrics) framework covers the full user lifecycle. It's the go-to structure for product case studies.

A
Acquisition
How do users find you? (SEO, ads, referrals)
A
Activation
First "aha" moment (signup, first action)
R
Retention
Do users come back? (DAU/MAU, cohort curves)
R
Revenue
How do you make money? (LTV, ARPU)
R
Referral
Do users invite others? (viral coefficient)
Interview Insight

When asked to "define metrics for product X," use AARRR as your skeleton. Pick 1-2 metrics per stage and explain which stage matters most for the company's current goals.

2 Diagnosing a Metric Drop

▾

When a metric drops, follow a structured debugging process. The first step is always checking if it's a data problem.

1
Data check
Is the data pipeline broken? Logging issues? Duplicate dedup?
2
External
Seasonality? Holiday? Competitor launch? App store changes?
3
Segment
Which user segment, platform, or region is affected?
4
Internal
Recent code deploy? Feature change? Experiment rollout?
5
Decompose
Break metric into components (e.g., revenue = users × orders/user × AOV)
Common Mistake

Jumping to "let's fix the product" before confirming the data is correct. Always rule out instrumentation issues first.

3 North Star Metrics

▾

A North Star metric captures the core value the product delivers to customers. It aligns the entire company.

CompanyNorth StarWhy
AirbnbNights bookedCaptures both supply and demand sides
FacebookDAUEngagement drives ad revenue
SpotifyTime spent listeningCore value proposition
SlackMessages sent per teamIndicates active adoption
UberRides completedMarketplace success
How to Choose

A good North Star metric: (1) reflects customer value, (2) is measurable, (3) is a leading indicator of revenue, (4) is actionable by multiple teams.

4 Market Sizing (Fermi Estimation)

▾

Break the problem into components, estimate each, and multiply. Show your reasoning, not just the answer.

StepActionExample: "How many piano tuners in Chicago?"
1Define scopeChicago metro area, ~2.7M households
2Estimate base~10% have pianos = 270K pianos
3Estimate frequencyTuned once/year = 270K tunings/year
4Estimate capacity1 tuner does ~4/day, 250 days = 1000/year
5Compute270K / 1000 = ~270 piano tuners
