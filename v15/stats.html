F1

Descriptive Statistics

+50 XP ▼

The mean is sensitive to outliers — a single billionaire drags the average. The median (middle value when sorted) is robust. The mode is the most frequent value, works for categorical data.

Variance measures spread via squared deviations. Standard deviation is √variance, same units as data. IQR (Q3 − Q1) captures the middle 50%, robust to outliers.

MeasureWhat It Tells YouSensitive ToWhen to Use
MeanAverage valueOutliers, skewSymmetric data
MedianMiddle valueNothing (robust)Skewed data
ModeMost frequent—Categorical data
Std DevSpread (same units)OutliersReporting spread
IQRMiddle 50% spreadNothing (robust)Outlier detection
Key Insight
Always ask "is my data skewed?" If yes, prefer median and IQR over mean and standard deviation. Using the wrong summary statistic is common, quiet damage.
F2

Hypothesis Testing

+75 XP ▼

The Framework:

1. Formulate H&sub0; (null) and H&sub1; (alternative)
2. Choose significance level α (0.05)
3. Collect data
4. Compute test statistic
5. Compare to critical value or compute p-value
6. Conclude: reject or fail to reject H&sub0;

The p-value is the probability of data this extreme assuming H&sub0; is true. It does NOT mean the probability H&sub0; is true. It does NOT mean the probability the result is due to chance.

ErrorNameAnalogyProbability
Type IFalse PositiveConvicting an innocentα (0.05)
Type IIFalse NegativeAcquitting a guiltyβ
PowerCorrect RejectionCatching the guilty1 − β (≥ 0.80)

Worked Example: New onboarding flow. Baseline 12%, observed 13.8% in 2,000 users. z = 2.48, p = 0.0066. p < 0.05 → reject H&sub0;: the new flow significantly increases conversions.

F4

Confidence Intervals

+75 XP ▼ 
CI = x̄ ± z* × (σ / √n)

z* values: 1.645 (90%), 1.96 (95%), 2.576 (99%)

Correct interpretation: If we repeated the sampling procedure 100 times, approximately 95 of those intervals would contain the true parameter. Confidence is in the procedure, not the single interval.

FactorEffect on WidthCan You Control?
Sample size nLarger → narrowerYes — bigger study
Variability σMore → widerNo (inherent)
Confidence level99% wider than 95%Yes (trade-off)

Worked Example: 200 sessions, mean = 4.2 min, SD = 1.8. SE = 1.8/√200 ≈ 0.127. Margin = 1.96 × 0.127 ≈ 0.249. 95% CI = (3.95, 4.45) minutes.

The hardest part of A/B testing isn't the statistics — it's defining the right metric. Spend as much time debating the metric as analyzing results.
F6

Central Limit Theorem

+100 XP ▼

Take any population with finite mean μ and standard deviation σ. Draw samples of size n. As n grows, the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution with mean = μ and SE = σ/√n.

The Intuition: Roll one die → uniform distribution. Roll 30 dice, average them → beautiful bell curve centered at 3.5. Works for any starting distribution. Rule of thumb: n ≥ 30 is usually sufficient.

Interactive: CLT Dice Simulator

Samples: 0
Key Insight
The Law of Large Numbers tells us sample means converge to the true mean. The CLT tells us how — normally, with SE = σ/√n. Together they form the foundation of statistical inference.
