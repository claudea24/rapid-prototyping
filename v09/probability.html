Core Concepts

Probability Axioms

Three fundamental rules: non-negativity, normalization, and additivity for mutually exclusive events.

Conditional Probability

Probability of A given B: P(A|B) = P(A∩B) / P(B). Foundation for Bayesian reasoning.

Bayes' Theorem

P(H|D) = P(D|H) × P(H) / P(D). Updates beliefs with new evidence.

Independence

Events A and B are independent if P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B). No influence between events.

Key Distributions

Discrete
Continuous

Binomial Distribution

For n independent trials with success probability p. PMF: P(X=k) = C(n,k) p^k (1-p)^(n-k)

Poisson Distribution

Models count of events in fixed interval. PMF: P(X=k) = e^(-λ) λ^k / k!

Normal Distribution

Bell-shaped curve, central to CLT. PDF: φ(x) = (1/√(2πσ²)) exp(-(x-μ)²/(2σ²))

Exponential Distribution

Models time between events. PDF: f(x) = λe^(-λx) for x ≥ 0
