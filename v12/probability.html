Section 1

Bayes' Theorem Calculator

Calculate the posterior probability P(A|B) given a prior, likelihood, and evidence. Pre-loaded with the classic medical test example: a 99% accurate test with 1% disease prevalence.

Posterior P(A|B)
0.1667
P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B) Step-by-step: P(A) = 0.01 (prior: 1% prevalence) P(B|A) = 0.99 (likelihood: 99% test accuracy) P(B) = 0.0594 (evidence: overall positive rate) P(A|B) = 0.99 * 0.01 / 0.0594 = 0.0099 / 0.0594 = 0.1667 (16.67%)
1Multiply likelihood by prior: P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.99 * 0.01 = 0.0099
2Divide by evidence: 0.0099 / 0.0594 = 0.1667
3Interpretation: Even with a 99% accurate test, a positive result only means a 16.67% chance of disease when prevalence is 1%.

Section 4

Birthday Problem

What's the probability that at least two people in a group share a birthday? The answer is surprisingly high -- only 23 people are needed for a >50% chance.

Probability of a Shared Birthday
50.73%
50.73%
P(at least one match) = 1 - P(all different) P(all different) = 365/365 * 364/365 * 363/365 * ... * (365-n+1)/365 For n = 23: P(all different) = 365! / (365^23 * (365-23)!) = 0.4927 P(match) = 1 - 0.4927 = 0.5073 (50.73%)
