🔬 Hypothesis Testing & P-Values FOCUS

▼

The Framework: (1) Formulate H0/H1, (2) Choose alpha, (3) Collect data, (4) Compute test statistic, (5) Compare to critical value, (6) Conclude.

What the p-value IS: P(data this extreme | H0 true).

What it is NOT: P(H0 is true), or "probability the result is due to chance."

Exercise: Interpret This P-Value

⏱ 00:00

An A/B test yields p = 0.03. Your PM says "there is only a 3% chance the feature has no effect." Is this correct? Why or why not?

Incorrect. The p-value is P(data this extreme | H0 true), NOT P(H0 true | data). It means: IF there were truly no effect, we would see results this extreme only 3% of the time. To claim "3% chance the null is true" you would need Bayes' theorem with a prior on H0, which frequentist statistics does not provide.

📈 Central Limit Theorem FOCUS

▼

Take any population with finite mean and variance. As sample size grows, the distribution of sample means approaches normal with mean = population mean and SE = sigma / sqrt(n).

Why it matters: Confidence intervals use x-bar plus/minus z*SE, which assumes x-bar is normal. P-values compare to the normal distribution. Without CLT, we would need to know the exact population distribution before making any inference.

Rule of thumb: n >= 30 is usually sufficient for mildly skewed data. More skewed populations need larger samples.

The 68-95-99.7 Rule: ~68% within 1 SD, ~95% within 2 SD, ~99.7% within 3 SD of the mean.

Key Insight: LLN tells us sample means converge to the true mean. CLT tells us how — normally, with SE = sigma/sqrt(n). Together they form the foundation of statistical inference.

Descriptive Stats

  • Skewed data: use median + IQR (not mean + SD)
  • Outlier detection: 1.5 x IQR rule

P-Value

  • P(data this extreme | H0 true)
  • NOT P(H0 is true | data)
  • Type I = false positive (alpha), Type II = false negative (beta)

A/B Testing

  • Peeking inflates FP from 5% to ~30%
  • Multiple comparisons: Bonferroni (alpha/n)
  • Always decide duration before starting
  • Segment by platform, geo, cohort

CLT

  • Sample means approach normal regardless of population shape
  • SE = sigma / sqrt(n)
  • n >= 30 usually sufficient
  • Foundation for CIs and hypothesis tests
